About Me

As a computer science teacher for the past 4 years, I came to realize not just how valuable skills like programming, web development, and data analysis could be, but how much I enjoyed doing them. I gained valuable experience in software development as I learned about how computers and the internet work, how files are formatted and sent across the physical internet, and the languages required to make it all happen. I was exposed to many different languages as I explored the best way to present it to my students in a way that was educational, but also would make them more interested in becoming developers themselves.


It also allowed me to see the same problem from many different perspectives as students would come up with different solutions or approaches. It was problem solving like this that drove me towards engineering at first, then teaching math and computer science, and now software development. It is my desire to learn and continuously solve problems that will lead me to be a better software developer.
